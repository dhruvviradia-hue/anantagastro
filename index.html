FAQ

Questions Before Your Visit

ERCP is a minimally invasive procedure using endoscopy and X-ray guidance to diagnose and treat bile duct stones, strictures and pancreatic duct problems. It is needed when imaging shows bile duct obstruction or when conservative treatment has failed.

See a gastroenterologist if acidity, gas or bloating is frequent, disturbs sleep or meals, causes vomiting, weight loss, black stool, blood in stool or keeps recurring after medicines.

ERCP is generally safe in experienced hands. The main risk is post-ERCP pancreatitis, which occurs in a small percentage. Dr. Dhruv uses evidence-based preventive protocols and explains risks clearly before each procedure.

Most bile leaks can be managed endoscopically through ERCP-guided stenting and sphincterotomy — avoiding the need for open surgery and allowing a faster recovery.

You will receive written bowel preparation instructions at the time of booking. Good preparation ensures the doctor can see the colon clearly and perform any needed interventions safely.

Seek urgent care for severe abdominal pain, vomiting blood, black tarry stool, rapid-onset jaundice, loss of consciousness or inability to keep any food or water down.
